  • Elyse Levine
    August 7, 2022

     Users of cryptocurrency who frequently use new or existing platforms are likely to receive an airdrop at some stage. Airdrops can be a result of blockchain-based projects or developers distributing free tokens to members of their communities as part of a broader marketing initiative.

    The idea is to distribute newly-minted tokens to hundreds or thousands of wallet addresses, with the intention that the users will feel more inclined to join the project, even if it's simply to discover how to cash out the tokens to fund some other thing.

    This concept is similar to receiving a discount card in your mailbox to encourage you to visit a new store within the region. However, a crypto airdrop does not primarily aim at making the recipient spend money but rather raising awareness for new services and projects.

    In most cases an airdrop of crypto is issued to users in exchange for a certain task. These tasks often involve things like:

    Follow an account via social media.

    Share or retweet one of their posts, with hashtags.

    - Transacting, or receiving a transaction (using an individual cryptocurrency platform or wallet).

    - Creating an account and joining to be notified of updates.

    There are also instances when crypto airdrops can be issued to users without having to take any action, as we'll discuss below.



    Why do crypto airdrops exist?


    The main reason to organize an airdrop of crypto is to promote a blockchain startup, project or service. By issuing tokens to users that allow the company to begin the process of launching its project and ensure a equitable distribution of tokens across its community from day one. Furthermore, those who receive these tokens are incentivized to spread awareness and aid in helping the project reach a wider market once the token is trading through an exchange. The more interest surrounding the token will be, the more likely it is to rise in price.

    Airdrops often gain traction by promoting the launch on the website, cryptocurrency forums and social media. This is similar to receiving the HelloFresh voucher in your email with a discount code and a coupon code, since these campaigns are intended to draw many more people to the platform with the incentive of money.

    The legitimate crypto airdrops do not ask users to make any investments. It is instead an opportunity to distinguish themselves from competitors that have pursued external funding before releasing their tokens.


    How do crypto airdrops work and are they secure?


    There are many ways to conduct an airdrop in crypto:

    - Request that users complete one or multiple small social tasks to be eligible to receive the airdrop at a later date.

    - Automatically distribute tokens to holders of a specific asset, and balances on the Blockchain on which the airdrop is scheduled to take place. (For instance, every address that has a equilibrium of 0.01 ether or more receives this airdrop.)

    A snapshot of the blockchain from a prior date and letting users access their airdrop tokens on the project's website through the use of a smart contract.

    Despite their popularity, crypto airdrops aren't always as risk-free as they seem.

    Because recipients receive "free cash" within their accounts, it is likely that there are bound to be airdrops which are nothing more than pump-and-dump schemes. In particular, the creator creates a token in the hope that there will be enough hype around it to be included on an exchange. Once tokens begin trading the creator then sells a large portion of tokens, which can cause a crash in the price.

    Another potential attack vector is the so-called dusting attack. A scammer will send an amount of money to an innocent user in order to break their privacy. Then, the criminal will trace the transactions made by the tokens of the wallet distributed to reveal the identity of the person or company operating the wallet.

    Finally, since airdrops are regarded as income according to the U.S. Internal Revenue Service This means that if you receive "free tokens" you'll need to pay tax on them, regardless of whether you were initially or not.


    How do you qualify for a crypto airdrop?


    Because a crypto airdrop is primarily designed for promotional and marketing purposes It can be difficult to know if you're qualified for future events. Each airdrop has specific requirements to assess whether you are eligible, however, the majority of them are simple to identify. A blockchain-based airdrop snapshot is usually announced following the fact, which makes qualification difficult. However there are alternative options to explore:

    - Find dedicated crypto airdrop websites (you typically receive a notification via email) - similar to signing up for promotions from the store you usually purchase from.

    Check your social media feeds for the #airdrop hashtag and check if any new developments have been happening.

    Be an active user of different cryptocurrency services, products platforms, and blockchains.

    - Explore cryptocurrency-related forums and news portals for mentions of a recent or upcoming airdrop.

    The primary requirement to receive crypto airdrops is having an account in a cryptocurrency wallet that has the balance. One can't receive or claim an airdrop without having an account with a wallet address. The use of an exchange address is convenient but is not appropriate for airdrops. Instead, users should consider the different digital wallets they can utilize to store their funds.

    Crypto users that frequently interact with new and existing platforms will likely receive an airdrop at some stage. Airdrops involve blockchain-based projects and developers sending out free tokens to members of their communities as part of a broader marketing initiative.


    The main idea is to send newly minted tokens to hundreds or thousands of different wallet addresses with the hope recipients will be more inclined to engage with the corresponding project — even if it’s only to learn how to cash out the free tokens into something else.


    This concept is similar to finding a free discount card in your mailbox to encourage you to visit a new store in the area. However, a crypto airdrop isn’t predominantly about making the recipient spend money but rather raising awareness for new projects and services.


    In most cases, a crypto airdrop is issued to users in exchange for completing a certain task. These tasks often involve things such as:



    • Following an account on social media.

    • Sharing or retweeting one of their posts, including hashtags.

    • Sending or receiving a transaction (using a particular crypto platform or wallet).

    • Creating an account and signing up to receive updates.


    There are also instances when a crypto airdrop may be issued to users without needing to do anything, as we will highlight below.


    Why do crypto airdrops exist?


    The primary reason for organizing a crypto airdrop is to promote a blockchain startup, project or service. By issuing tokens to users, the team can bootstrap its project and ensure a fair distribution of tokens among its community from day one. Moreover, recipients of these tokens are incentivized to increase awareness and help the project reach a broader audience once the token begins trading on an exchange. The more interest surrounding the token, the more likely it will rise in price.


    Airdrops often gain traction by promoting the launch on a project’s website, cryptocurrency forums and social media. These campaigns are designed to bring more people to the platform through a financial incentive.


    Legitimate crypto airdrops will never ask users to make any investment. Instead, it is a way to stand out among competitors that have pursued external funding before issuing their tokens.
