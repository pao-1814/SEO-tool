Aave VS BlockFi: Which Is The Best Crypto Landing Platform?

Adam White
Comparison

In recent years, crypto lending platforms have become an additional way to make money in the cryptocurrency market. Instead of holding cryptocurrencies until there is a significant increase in their prices, you can lend your digital assets to borrowers and earn interest.

But which crypto lending platform to choose? In this article, we will compare the two most popular platforms — Aave and BlockFi — in terms of interest rates, tokens supported, and fees. So read on to find the best option for yourself!

What Is Aave Crypto Lending Platform?

Unlike other cryptocurrency lending sites, Aave is a decentralized platform. That means it doesn’t act as an intermediary between lenders and borrowers, so you lend directly to borrowers on the platform. Using blockchain technology verifies transactions and provides users with security and transparency. Aave features include:

  • The open-source software allows users to create their applications on the platform
  • No blocking period or deposit limits
  • Total users’ control over the platform  — members can vote on important governance issues

What Is BlockFi Crypto Lending Platform?

BlockFi was founded in 2017 to provide lending services to markets where there was no access to simple financial products. BlockFi is a centralized crypto lending platform supporting all major tokens and a wide range of stablecoins. BlockFi’s main product is its savings account, which allows users to earn by lending their crypto assets.

When it comes to BlockFi safety, it’s essential to understand that crypto accounts on BlockFi are not protected by the FDIC or SIPC.

Aave VS Block Fi: Interest Rates

When depositing your funds into Aave to become a lender, you can earn interest at up to 16.23% APR depending on the crypto.

BlockFi crypto interest rates vary from 0.10% to 10% depending on the currency and deposit amount. For example, depositing up to 0.1 BTC assumes a 3.50% Annual Percentage Yield (APY). With the DOT coin, you can earn interest at a 10% APY regardless of the deposit amount.

BlockFi VS Aave: Tokens Supported

There are several markets on Aave covering various Ethereum tokens and stablecoins. Loans and credits on the Aave platform can be backed by tokens like Tether, DAI, Chainlink, MakerDAO, and Uniswap. It is noteworthy that Aave was the first lending platform to allow users to borrow and lend USDT.

BlockFi supports all major tokens and a wide range of stablecoins. BTC, ETH, PAXG, or LTC can be used as collateral.

BlockFi VS Aave: Fees Applied

BlockFi charges withdrawal fees when transferring funds from your Wallet account to an external account. For example, withdrawing up to 1,000,000 per 7-day period in USDT involves a $25 fee.

As for Aave, the platform charges users the following fees:

  • The borrowing fee of 0.09% per annum for ETH-based assets
  • The lending fee of 0.04%
  • The fee for urgent loans of 0.09%

Deposit and withdrawal fees on Aave are usually below $1.

How Do Aave And BlockFi Compare To Crypto.com?

BlockFi VS Crypto.com

The key differences between BlockFi and Crypto.com can be found in interest rates, tokens supported, fees, and other features.

When it comes to interest rates, BlockFi offers users a range of options with APY up to 10%, depending on the currency and amount deposited. Meanwhile, Crypto.com allows users to earn up to 14.50% APY on their deposits when staking at least $40,000 in CRO.

BlockFi supports all major tokens, a wide range of stablecoins, and the U.S. dollar as a fiat currency. Crypto.com offers an even greater array of tokens for users to invest in, plus over 20 fiat currencies.

Next, BlockFi charges no transaction fees on trades, while Crypto.com does. The fees charged by Crypto.com depend on the number of trades executed in 30 days.

In addition, Crypto.com has a much wider range of products and services. The ones that BlockFi lacks include cryptocurrency cards to debit accounts for easy money management and NFT exchange.

Lastly, Crypto.com has an extensive rewards program that allows users to earn cashback on all purchases with the platform’s native token CRO. BlockFi does not offer such features.

Aave VS Crypto.com

Regarding interest rates, Aave allows users to earn up to 16.23% APR on their deposits, while Crypto.com offers 14.50% APY on staking of at least $40,000 in CRO.

Both Aave and Crypto.com support an extensive array of tokens plus fiat currencies. With regards to fees, Aave charges borrowing and lending fees, whereas Crypto.com imposes trading fees.

Moreover, Crypto.com has a much wider range of products and services, including cryptocurrency cards for easy money management, an NFT exchange as well as a rewards program.

Bottom Line

Comparing platforms for lending cryptocurrencies is a daunting task. Each platform has its own strengths and weaknesses that matter when making an informed decision. Aave and BlockFi are two popular crypto lending platforms offering comparable interest rates and completely different approaches in terms of decentralization. Ultimately, it is up to each user to decide which platform best suits their needs depending on the deposit amount and their investment strategies.

FAQ

What is Aave?

Aave is a decentralized lending platform that lets borrowing and landing cryptos without a centralized intermediary.

How do Aave and BlockFi differ?

Aave is a decentralized platform, meaning it is not owned or controlled by a single entity, while BlockFi is a centralized platform owned and operated by a company. Additionally, Aave focuses primarily on lending and borrowing, while BlockFi offers various services, including trading, borrowing, and interest-earning accounts.

Which platform has better interest rates?

Interest rates on both platforms can vary depending on the cryptocurrency and the market conditions. Thus, it’s important to compare rates for the specific cryptocurrency you are interested in. However, in general, BlockFi tends to offer slightly higher interest rates than Aave.

Which platform is safer to use?

Both platforms have their own risks and benefits, but both are generally considered reputable and safe to use. However, as with any cryptocurrency platform, it’s important to do your research and ensure you understand the risks involved.
