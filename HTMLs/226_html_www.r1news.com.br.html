However, with Bitcoin loans, you get the money within 24 hours. So, if you are looking for money for an emergency, Bitcoin loans are the best option.

Flexibility of loan conditions

When it comes to payment terms, Bitcoin loans are flexible compared to traditional loan. The borrower decides on the length of the loan, the loan-to-value ratio and the currency in which he wishes to be repaid.

Explain Bitcoin Loans

Bitcoin loans have increased during the pandemic. Many people have started looking for ways in which their digital assets could benefit them. Let us explain Bitcoin loans to you with an example:

Here, Crypto lending platforms will come to its rescue. He can use these Bitcoins as collateral and get a stable coin loan.

This acts as a kind of insurance in situations if the price of Bitcoin drops sharply. But it can have a negative impact on the borrower, especially if their platform expects them to maintain the loan-to-value ratio.

How to get a Bitcoin loan

Now that we have understood what a bitcoin loan is, it is time to analyze how to get one. There are two types of platforms for obtaining a Bitcoin loan.

Centralized lending platform

Decentralized lending platform

Some examples of centralized lending platforms are BlockFi, Nexo, and Binance, and they want you to follow specific rules to get the loan. You will first need to create an account on these platforms. Then you need to complete the ‚ÄúKnow Your Customer‚Äù formalities for the loan to be processed.

These platforms also have protocols to ensure that the warranty remains secure. Some protect BTC through insurance, while others keep the majority of them in digital storage. Centralized crypto lending platforms always record deposits and withdrawals.

The proposed interest rate is around 8%, which is relatively high compared to what conventional banks offer. There is also more paperwork involved in this matter. But since these platforms are better regulated, borrowers find them a better option.

On the other hand, the Decentralized lending platforms have transactions processed by codes and not by people. In the case of such platforms, smart contracts use algorithms to process loans and automate the process. Anyone can access the protocols of these platforms. There is no middleman or regulator, so there is no KYC process here.

The only downside here is that the interest rates offered are lower than what a centralized platform can offer. Obtaining any type of loan from these platforms is quite simple. You don‚Äôt have to provide any papers. All the client does is apply for the loan, donate the crypto, and the loan is processed through smart contracts.

Users of decentralized platforms can apply for a loan of any amount. They do not have to confirm their identity to anyone. On specific decentralized and centralized platforms, the user can simply open an account using crypto, take out loans, or trade tokens.

Conclusion

The use of Bitcoin is increasing every day. As the pandemic has entered the picture, the need to borrow against this cryptocurrency has increased. However, it will take some time before this concept is universally accepted.

No one knows how things will turn out in the future. Both platforms have their share of advantages and disadvantages. It is purely the user‚Äôs decision what he wants to do.

In the years to come, BTC will become an accepted currency around the world. Until then, we have to let things move at their own pace. Hopefully, these transformations will benefit customers and platforms in a balanced way.
