Are economists still critical of crypto?

  1. Economist
    4d6a

    Blockchain is fine, but there is this problem that mining indeed aggravates global warming, even though it really is not a currency. Note how most of those defending crypto do so on the grounds of how much money they have personally made on it. So it may be a great speculative asset, or at least a highly volatile one, which makes it possible both to make a lot of money and lose a lot of money, although we do not hear from the people experiencing the latter.

    Some crptos do have some other potential uses, notably ethereum compared to bitcoin, althiough both of them, and pretty much the rest of them, have not remotely been used for the various things they might supposedly be used for. None of them are actually remotely functioning as media of exchange except for illegal transactions or some large international transfers.

    Blockchain, however, has a lot of uses and is increasingly being used well beyond any cryptocurrencies. Those defending cryptos on the basis of the usefulness of blockchains are the ones out of it not knowing what they are talking about.
